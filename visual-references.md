# Stage 1 Final: Visual Direction

Status: Finalized

This document defines the confirmed visual direction for Alex's personal portfolio website. It should guide later information architecture, content writing, design system creation, and implementation.

Do not treat the reference site as a layout to clone. Use it as a mood and visual language reference only.

## Main Visual Reference

Primary reference: https://diegovz.com/

Confirmed direction:

- Dark editorial homepage
- Oversized white typography
- Subtle portrait integrated into the background
- Minimal accent color
- Early selected projects
- Recruiter-readable content
- Lightweight interactions only

The portfolio should feel confident, modern, memorable, and professional. It should not feel like a generic resume site, a SaaS landing page, a student template, or a visual designer portfolio.

## Final Visual Direction

The site should use a dark editorial visual system built around strong typography, a restrained portrait treatment, and clear project evidence.

Core visual qualities:

- Dark, polished, and editorial
- High-contrast but readable
- Strong first-screen identity
- Calm and professional
- Minimal decorative UI
- Recruiter-friendly structure
- Portfolio-ready presentation

The first impression should communicate:

- Alex has a clear professional direction.
- Alex can connect PR, Marketing Communications, Content Operations, and AI Workflow work.
- Alex has concrete projects and experience, not only broad interests.
- The site is visually memorable without hiding important content.

## Homepage Section Order

Use this homepage structure:

1. Hero
2. Selected Projects
3. Proof Points
4. Experience
5. Education
6. About
7. Contact

Projects must appear early. The homepage should not spend too long on biography before showing evidence.

## Hero Design Rules

The hero section should carry the strongest visual identity.

Rules:

- Use black or near-black as the main hero background.
- Use oversized white typography as the dominant visual element.
- Keep the headline short, sharp, and role-relevant.
- Include a concise positioning line for PR, Marketing Communications, Content Operations, and AI Workflow roles.
- Include 3-4 capability tags.
- Include lightweight actions such as View Projects and Contact.
- Make the hero feel confident, not vague or overly poetic.
- Keep important text readable across desktop and mobile.

Avoid:

- Long self-introductions in the hero
- Generic claims such as "passionate about communication"
- Large decorative shapes or flashy gradients
- Hero text that is hard to read over the portrait
- A layout that looks like a fashion editorial more than a portfolio

## Portrait Usage Rules

Use Alex's original portrait only.

Do not alter:

- Facial features
- Face shape
- Expression
- Eyes
- Nose
- Lips
- Skin structure
- Hair shape
- Clothing

Allowed treatment:

- Grayscale
- Dark overlay
- Opacity adjustment
- Contrast adjustment
- Subtle blur only if needed for background integration
- Gradient mask
- CSS-style blending into the black background

Portrait rules:

- The portrait should support the hero identity, not dominate the page.
- The portrait should feel integrated into the dark background.
- The face should remain recognizable, but not overly bright or profile-photo-like.
- Use the portrait as atmosphere and identity, not as a standalone headshot card.
- The treatment should be reversible and CSS-like wherever possible.

Avoid:

- AI-changing the face
- AI-changing the expression
- AI-changing hairstyle or facial structure
- Hard rectangular portrait placement in the hero
- Overly bright portrait lighting against the black background

## Language Strategy

The site should be bilingual with separate language versions.

Routes:

- `/en` for the English version
- `/zh` for the Chinese version

Language switcher:

- EN
- 中文

Rules:

- Do not mix English and Chinese paragraph by paragraph on the same page.
- The two language versions should share the same visual design, layout, components, and project structure.
- The copy should be localized rather than translated word for word.
- Keep the bilingual system visually lightweight.
- Do not let the language switcher become a major visual element.

English version:

- Target international-facing roles.
- Emphasize overseas PR, Marketing Communications, Content Operations, AI Workflow, and foreign-company contexts.
- Use concise, professional English copy.

Chinese version:

- Target China-based recruiters, domestic PR / Marketing Communications roles, PR agencies, content operations, outbound brands, and AI content workflow roles.
- Strengthen Chinese context around Xiaohongshu-style content, domestic content platforms, and Chinese recruitment expectations.
- Give proper Chinese context to Cover News / 封面新闻 and Sichuan Radio and Television / 四川广播电视台.
- Explain Haidilao and Mixue examples in a way that fits domestic marketing and content expectations.

## Selected Projects Rules

Selected Projects should appear immediately after the Hero.

Primary projects:

- LUMA Coffee PR Campaign
- Coze Ecommerce Content Workflow
- Cover News International News Internship
- New Zealand Bilingual Site Coordination

Each project preview should include:

- Project title
- Project type
- One-sentence value summary
- Alex's role
- Skills demonstrated
- Main deliverables or outputs
- Link to detail page when available

Design rules:

- Use a clean editorial card/list hybrid style.
- Keep project cards readable and scannable.
- Prioritize text clarity over decorative imagery.
- Use images, diagrams, or document previews only when they improve credibility.
- Make the target-role relevance obvious without requiring the recruiter to open every detail page.

Avoid:

- Image-only project tiles
- Decorative cards with unclear content
- Too many projects on the homepage
- Hiding important project information behind hover states

## Proof Points Rules

Proof Points should show concrete evidence of ability.

Use achievement-style signals rather than generic deliverable lists.

Examples:

- 40% faster news lead evaluation
- 30 min to 5-10 min morning briefing SOP
- Full PR materials package for LUMA Coffee
- Lightweight ecommerce content workflow built in Coze
- Bilingual coordination in a New Zealand worksite

Rules:

- Keep each proof point short and scannable.
- Connect each proof point to a target role or capability.
- Use specific outcomes, workflows, artifacts, or performance signals.
- Be careful with numbers that are estimated or not verified.
- If a metric is not final, phrase it conservatively during content writing.

Avoid:

- Vague traits such as "hard-working" or "creative"
- Long paragraphs
- Unverified claims that sound inflated
- A list that repeats the project cards without adding new evidence

## Experience And Education Rules

Experience should support the target roles and show credibility quickly.

Relevant experience may include:

- Cover News / 封面新闻
- Sichuan Radio and Television / 四川广播电视台
- New Zealand bilingual site coordination
- Massey University experience and coursework

Rules:

- Keep entries concise.
- Use role, organization, location, dates, and 2-4 focused bullets.
- Prioritize communication, content, coordination, research, reporting, and workflow experience.
- Avoid turning the homepage into a full resume.

Education should be clear but not over-expanded unless it directly supports positioning.

## Interaction Rules

Allowed:

- Subtle fade-in
- Gentle project hover states
- Smooth anchor scrolling
- Light opacity or contrast changes
- Minimal section reveal

Avoid:

- Scroll hijacking
- Heavy motion graphics
- Game-like interaction
- Complex cursor effects
- Interactions that hide or delay important content
- Animations that make the site feel like a visual designer portfolio
- Motion that reduces mobile readability

Interactions should support rhythm and clarity. Recruiters should be able to read the site quickly without learning how to use it.

## Things To Avoid

- Do not clone Diego Vz exactly.
- Do not copy the reference layout one-to-one.
- Do not make the site feel like a senior visual designer portfolio.
- Do not hide key career information behind animation.
- Do not overuse motion graphics.
- Do not use cyberpunk styling.
- Do not use flashy gradients.
- Do not create a generic SaaS template feeling.
- Do not create a student-project feeling.
- Do not bury selected projects below a long About section.
- Do not mix English and Chinese paragraph by paragraph on the same page.
- Do not use AI-edited facial changes for the portrait.
